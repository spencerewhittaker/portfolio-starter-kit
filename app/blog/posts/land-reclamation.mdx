Historically, countries have drained swamps, built artificial islands, and dammed water to expand their territory. 
But it seems like this has all but stopped. 
Land reclamation involves complex feats of engineering, urban planning, and political maneuvering. 

We’re interested in publishing anything about this – from a granular look at a specific project of sufficient scale, to a piece about what political, economic, and scientific conditions allow such projects to happen, to a concrete proposal for making land reclamation projects viable in high-value places.
